Project Ideas

A project idea that I would probably be passionate about could be Web Development. I'd develop a website that could handle multiple media types which have their own playback applications as well as storage and management. It could work as a "Video on Demand" but with a strong use of Open Source repositories and systems. It'll prove useful for me because it will reinforce what I currently understand in programming as well explore something new along the way, and the website will prove useful to other users because they will be able to contribute to the website in its functions and technicalities.

There are other competing websites that already cater this kind of area, such as YouTube, Twitch and Vimeo. In this area YouTube is the most dominant, especially with their online video services and content and YouTube channels, and then there's Twitch coming in second for their streaming services.

Developing an Open-Source website is a great idea in terms of developing and maintaining a community. Where YouTube is a corporation and Twitch's communities mainly consist of viewers and subscribers/donors = an Open-Source alternative would mean that the website is fully community-driven, from the development of the website to the services and third-party applications.

Generally in the development of a website CSS, HTML and JavaScript are industry tools and are the more popular combination of tools, but for my website I want to probably use the Ruby on Rails framework alongside HTML, CSS and C-language in order to develop a website. Ruby as a programming language is developed in C-language, and with the Rails framework developing and deploying a website could be faster than the traditional use of JavaScript-HTML-CSS combo.

In terms of skills: Web development can be a self-taught area of expertise. Knowing what programming language, frameworks, libraries, etcetera, are what separates an amateur from a veteran.

IF the project is successful, it would probably be flooded by other web developers who'd want to be a part of the website's future, thus over-populating a once small server, meaning we'll be needing a larger system to handle all the incoming traffic.

The impact of this development may open up the eyes of few corporate entities such as YouTube and Twitch in understanding the need in developing a community and maintaining a community.

Social Engineering, Penetration Tester, Security Specialist

. . . are a part of Information Security. Social Engineering is mainly summarised as the 'manipulation of people' or 'psychological manipulation' this is done in a way for users or any suspect/victim to be unknowingly directed to perform certain actions or a desired consequence in order for them to give up confidential (private) information. Social Engineering is a way to gather information, work with what you gather and be able to provide security for your network, software or other kinds of computer-systems. Companies will hire a Social Engineer to protect their private information. What has interested me in this field is the gathering information, penetrating defences and the psychological understanding of how an attacker will infiltrate, what they'll do with insecure parts of a system and how they use technological manipulation. You, as a penetration tester will work in teams in capture the flag scenarios but with information. So you also have teams who are Blue Teams (defenders), Red Teams (attackers) and Purple Teams (attackers and defenders). At some point, the public will need to be educated, not just on the basics of protecting private information but also in how to protect themselves, translating from the cyber world to the real world.

The Required Skills for the Position

-​ Be able to identify and design and implement the required security within a project. -​ Document a report about your findings and solutions in a recreatable and thorough step by step guide. -​ Develop software/programs/applications for security. -​ Experience and knowledge with industry tools. -​ Security Architecture and Designs. -​ An Understanding of the internals of the Linux Operating System.
